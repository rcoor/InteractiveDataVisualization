Online interactive visualizations

Among the Oscar Contenders, a Host of Connections A visualization showing actors connections to oscar nominated movies, for New York Times by Mike Bostock.

Positive: this visualization has used a clever way of linking oscar nominated actors to movies that has recieved an oscar. Linking multiple data points together can be relatively complex. The author has put the actor in focus as big icons. It is clear that the actors are in focus. Having movies as connected nodes makes it possible to have multiple actors connected to the same node and in turn figuring out who have worked on the same movie.

Negative: before understand excactly what was going on I had to read a few boxes of text. There is a lot to take in.

Each Country's Share of CO2 Emissions A diagram showing each countries percentage of CO2 emissions from energy consumption, by Union of Concerned Scientiests.

Positive: a simple representation pie chart shows a division of different countries. There's a power strong popup effect as it is easy to see which slice is bigger than the other. It works well as the emphasis is on the size of emission.

Negative: there is an popout effect however this effect becomes smaller when you look at the countries with least emissions. It's hard to see a difference between countries that have below 2%. With the few amounts of countries representated it might have been better to show a simple bar chart, which would have made it easier to spot differences even in countries with low CO2 emissions.

What I want to build

Theis Jakobsen

Data: I have datafiles with 250.000 SNPs on 150 people. Some of these SNPs have been linked to 20 categories which have a certain phenotype in fitness, nutrition and lifestyle. Each category have a score that goes from 0 to 100. I would like to visualise, for each category and all categories combined, a comparison between one persons unique cateogory result and the mean of peoples result.

Story: I want the visualisation to tell a story about the unique individual. Where are you compared to other people in this category and all categories combined. It should allow the person to dive into his own genetics and explore him-/herself.

Audience: The audience is people who get a gene test who are interested in learning more about themself. It draws a unqiue image that is different from person to person.

Sketch: One of the reasons that I have taken this course is to be inspired to help me find out what kind of visualisation I want to use on the describe data.

By Theis Jakobsen for the course Interactive Data Exploration, 2016.
